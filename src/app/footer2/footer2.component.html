Parkway Derby Limited is an Appointed Representative of Automotive Compliance Ltd, which is authorised and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA No. 497010). Their role allows Parkway Derby Limited to act as a credit broker (not a lender), for introducing a limited number of lenders, and also act on behalf of the insurer for insurance distribution activities. They are a credit broker, not a lender, and at no point act as an agent or independent financial advisor. Their approach is to first introduce you to the manufacturer lender linked directly to the franchise you’re purchasing your vehicle from, who typically offers the best available package regarding interest rates and contributions. If that lender can’t make you an offer, they will then introduce you to another lender on their panel to secure a suitable finance agreement. Parkway Derby Limited earns a commission from the lender for making the introduction, which can be either fixed or a percentage of the amount you borrow. If the funding is via your vehicle purchase, different lenders pay different commission structures, and some franchise lenders also provide referral rates to fund marketing and training. They clarify that these commission payments from lenders do not impact the amount you’ll repay under your finance agreement. However, you will be contributing toward the commission paid to them via interest collected on repayments. Before proposing finance to a potential lender, Parkway Derby Limited will inform you of the likely commission amount and seek your consent to receive it. The exact commission rate you’ll receive is confirmed before signing your agreement. All finance applications are subject to status, terms and conditions apply, UK residents only, aged 18 or over. They also note that guarantees may be required.
